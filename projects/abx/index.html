Experiment Analysis A/B/x

What problem I was solving

Product teams need clear ship-or-don’t-ship decisions, not just p-values. I analyzed a real A/B/x experiment to mirror how growth and game teams actually make calls.

What I did

Using mobile game retention data, I compared multiple variants to control, applied variance reduction with CUPED, monitored performance over time, and evaluated lift with proper confidence bounds.

What I found

CUPED materially reduced variance, improving the ability to detect meaningful retention differences. The analysis led to a defensible recommendation instead of an ambiguous result.

Why it matters

  • Experimental design beyond textbook A/B tests.
  • Noise reduction to improve decision quality.
  • Translating statistical output into product recommendations.
