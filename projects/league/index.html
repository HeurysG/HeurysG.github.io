League of Legends Match Outcome

What problem I was curious about

League of Legends matches feel chaotic, but they are not. I wanted to see if outcomes could be predicted reliably from objectives and team stats—and which actions actually decide games.

What I did

Cleaned and explored match data, then trained ensemble models (Gradient Boosting and Random Forests) on team objectives and performance features, using cross-validation to avoid overfitting.

What I found

Gradient Boosting hit ~97% accuracy, with Random Forests close behind. Objective control—especially turrets and inhibitors—dominated win prediction, far outweighing most individual stat noise.

Why it matters

  • Translate a messy, real-world dataset into a clear prediction task.
  • Choose appropriate models and evaluate them rigorously.
  • Explain why the model works, not just report accuracy.
